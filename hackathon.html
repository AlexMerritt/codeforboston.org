About

The Code for Boston Civic Hackathon is an event spanning the weekend of April 6th and 7th, designed to bring together passionate individuals with the goal of using technology to solve social and civic problems in the greater Boston community. Code for Boston is a Code for America brigage made up of developers, designers, data scientists, passionate citizens, and more.

FAQ

What is a civic hackathon?
A civic hackathon is a event spanning an entire weekend where attendees from all walks of life come together to innovate solutions to problems in the greater Boston area. There are no prizes, but only the opportunity to collaborate and dive deep into the issues that impact our community.
Who can participate?
Anyone with an interest in finding ways to leverage technology to address problems facing individuals in our community. You don't need to be a developer or tech enthused person to attend - civic hacking is about coding as much as it is about fostering relationships between government, nonprofit, academic, for-profit companies, residents, civic technologists, analysts, designers, and more.
How much does it cost?
The Code for Boston Civic Hackathon is free to attend, and includes meals and snacks provided throughout the event.
